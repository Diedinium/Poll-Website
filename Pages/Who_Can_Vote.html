Who can vote?

This page will explore who is actually allowed to vote in the UK

A quick answer: Most people over 18.


We say "quick" because it's not really quite that simple; in theory if you are over 18 and living in the UK then you should be allowed to vote, however there are some other requirements that you need to meet in order to be allowed to vote in general elections:

  • Must be a British, Irish or Commonwealth Citizen
  • Be a resident at an address in the UK (Or in the last 15 years if living aborad)
  • Not be legally excluded from voting

As you can see, generally if you are a young person in the UK you will already meet these requirements. There are a list of criteria that makes people illegible to vote as well:

  • If they are a member of the House of Lords
  • EU citizens who are residing in the UK
  • Anyone other than British, Irish or Commonwealth qualifying citizens
  • Convicted persons serving sentences
  • Anyone found guilty within the previous five years of corrupt or illegal practices in connection with an election

So long as you are over 18, and meet eligibility requirements then there should be no reason why you are not allowed to vote.

Something important to remember...


Remeber to register!


None of what has been said here matters if you don't register to vote! Make sure you complete the registration process on the .gov website, otherwise you may not be able to vote.
